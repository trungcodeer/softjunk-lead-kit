Answers:

Question: What should I text a lead after a missed call?
Answer: Name the missed call, offer help, and ask for one easy next step.
Sample: Hi [Name], sorry I missed your call about [service]. I can help. Do you want the fastest available time, or should I answer one quick question first?

Question: How do I follow up after sending an estimate?
Answer: Reference the estimate, remove friction, and ask whether price, timing, or scope is blocking the decision.
Sample: Hi [Name], checking that you saw the estimate for [project]. If the scope looks right, I can send the next step. If price or timing is the blocker, reply with that and I will adjust the path.

Question: How do I reply when a lead says the price is too high?
Answer: Acknowledge the concern and offer a smaller or clearer next step instead of arguing.
Sample: Totally fair to compare options. If budget is the main issue, I can show the smallest useful version first or explain what is included so you can decide.

Question: What should I send after a no-show?
Answer: Keep the tone neutral and make rescheduling easy.
Sample: Hi [Name], looks like today got busy. Do you want me to send another time for [service], or should I close this for now?

Question: How do I follow up with a quiet DM lead?
Answer: Use a low-pressure reply with a one-word response option.
Sample: Hi [Name], quick follow-up on [service]. If you are still interested, reply with "times" and I will send the easiest next step. If not, no problem.
